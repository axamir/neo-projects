# Investment Model

Version: 0.1

Status: Living Document

---

# Overview

Lewis OS is designed to help projects become more credible before significant capital is requested.

The goal is not to ask for large funding too early.

The goal is to reduce uncertainty step by step until the project can justify its next level of investment.

---

# The Investment Ladder

Lewis OS organizes venture development into progressive levels.

## Level 1 — Preparation

Typical starting point:

**3 × $96.3**

Purpose:

- clarify the idea
- define the project
- prepare it for presentation

This level creates the minimum credible body of work needed for a serious next decision.

---

## Level 2 — Prototype

Indicative starting point:

**$963**

Purpose:

- create a visual or functional demonstration
- test the project narrative
- validate core user flows
- produce a stronger presentation asset

This level is not a full product.

It is evidence that the concept can be experienced.

---

## Level 3 — MVP

Indicative starting point:

**$9,630**

Purpose:

- build the first usable product
- implement core functionality
- create a private or limited pilot
- collect real user feedback
- validate technical and commercial assumptions

The exact budget must be estimated from the approved architecture.

---

## Level 4 — Growth

Indicative starting point:

**$96,300**

Purpose:

- improve infrastructure
- expand the team
- strengthen security and reliability
- grow distribution
- build sales and support systems
- prepare for larger-scale investment

This level is only relevant after real evidence exists.

---

# When Should Investors Enter?

An investor should not enter simply because an idea sounds exciting.

Investment becomes more credible when the project has:

- clear ownership
- a defined problem
- an identifiable market
- a documented solution
- realistic costs
- an approved roadmap
- evidence of demand
- and a capable execution path

Lewis OS prepares projects to reach that point.

---

# Funding Sources

A project may advance through different sources of capital:

- founder funding
- client revenue
- grants
- strategic partners
- angel investment
- venture capital
- revenue-sharing agreements
- marketplace acquisition
- or a combination of these

The recommended source depends on project maturity and risk.

---

# Lewis OS Compensation

Lewis OS may earn through:

- fixed preparation fees
- prototype or build fees
- priority delivery fees
- project-management fees
- marketplace commission
- licensing
- white-label services
- approved revenue share
- or separately negotiated equity

No equity, ownership, or revenue share is assumed automatically.

Every such arrangement requires a separate written agreement.

---

# Transparent Budget Structure

For selected future projects, Lewis OS may use a transparent allocation model.

Example:

- 30% — architecture, management, documentation, and studio fee
- 70% — direct execution expenses

Direct execution expenses may include:

- AI and API credits
- development tools
- hosting
- design
- contractors
- testing
- legal work
- deployment
- and approved marketing experiments

This model should be supported by a project ledger and milestone-based reporting.

---

# Risk Principle

The next investment level should only open when the previous stage has produced enough evidence.

Capital should follow clarity.

Clarity should follow evidence.

Evidence should follow completed work.

---

# Core Commitment

Lewis OS does not promise that every project will attract investment.

It prepares projects so funding decisions can be made with greater clarity, stronger evidence, and lower avoidable risk.
